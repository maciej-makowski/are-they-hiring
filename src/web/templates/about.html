What is this?

This site is a satirical tracker on AI-hype predictions. In March 2025, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei claimed AI would be writing essentially all code within a year. The counter on the home page tracks how much of that window has elapsed.

What it shows

Every few hours we scrape the public job boards of six AI labs and count how many of today's listings are generic software engineering roles. The home page reports today's number. The 30-day calendar shows the trail.

The companies currently tracked:

  • Anthropic
  • OpenAI
  • Google DeepMind
  • xAI
  • Perplexity
  • Meta (FAIR)

How classification works

Each job title is fed through a local LLM (qwen2.5:1.5b via Ollama) with a narrow rule: generic product, backend, frontend, mobile, and SRE roles count. AI / research / ML engineering, infrastructure, security, management, and sales do not. The idea is to isolate the kind of role the prediction was about — the commodity software engineer — rather than specialised AI work.

For the exact prompt and rule list see Implementation.md.

Caveats

  • Sample bias. Greenhouse / Ashby / metacareers JSON is what each company publicly exposes, which is not the whole hiring picture.
  • The classifier is not perfect. Internal benchmarking puts precision at roughly 70%, and it occasionally mislabels edge cases.
  • Only six companies. A broader sample would be more convincing, but also more work to maintain.
  • This is not a prediction market or a serious forecast. It is a countdown with a scoreboard.
